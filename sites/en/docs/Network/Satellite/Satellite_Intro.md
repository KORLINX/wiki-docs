KORLINX satellite modules provide IoT connectivity anywhere on Earth — critical for remote, off-grid, or mobile deployments where terrestrial networks are unavailable or unreliable.

## Satellite Orbit Types

| Orbit | Altitude | Latency | Data Rate | Operators |
|-------|----------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **LEO** (Low Earth Orbit) | 500–2000 km | 20–40 ms | Up to Mbps | Starlink, Iridium, Globalstar, Kinéis |
| **MEO** (Medium Earth Orbit) | 2000–36,000 km | 100–200 ms | Moderate | O3b (SES) |
| **GEO** (Geostationary Orbit) | 35,786 km | 500–700 ms | High (broadband) | Inmarsat, Viasat, HughesNet |

---

## LEO vs GEO for IoT

### LEO
- **Low latency** — suitable for real-time telemetry and two-way communication
- **Lower cost per message** — emerging constellations driving prices down
- **Growing coverage** — expanding constellations improve reliability
- **Best for**: asset tracking, remote monitoring, emergency communications

### GEO
- **Always available** — stationary satellite, simple antenna pointing
- **Mature technology** — decades of deployment, well-understood
- **Higher latency** — not suitable for real-time control
- **Best for**: one-way data reporting, wide-area broadcast

---

## Key Applications

- **Maritime**: vessel tracking, fleet management, crew communications
- **Aviation**: aircraft telemetry, EFB connectivity
- **Energy**: offshore platform monitoring, pipeline telemetry
- **Agriculture**: precision farming in remote fields
- **Emergency Response**: connectivity when terrestrial infrastructure fails
